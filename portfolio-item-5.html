Stroke Prediction Model

R Programming based prediction model

According to WHO, stroke is the 2nd leading cause of deaths globally and therefore it is imperative that technology is better utilized to better investigate the multitude of variables.

I first visualized the relationships between the stroke variable and the predictor variables such as age, smoking, hypertension etc. Exploratory Data Visualization helped me identify the patterns and relationships between the variables. Pearson's Correlation heatmap allowed me to quickly visualise the associations between different variables. The heatmap showed some associaton between age, bmi and stroke. Utilizing ggplot, I visualized the relationshio between bmi, age and stroke. The chances of stroke increase with age howevere the plot also shows that around bmi of 50 and as age increases, there are more chances of getting stroke.

Logistic Regression was used to build the model since getting a stroke or not is binary, 0 or 1. The data was divided into training and testing daatframe variables. The p value showed that age was statiscally signifant. I also used randomForest as an alternative model.

Before conducting the correlation and the regression model, I visualozed the relationships between variables and the if patient had stroke or not. These visualizations serve as a lens through which I can see pattersn and the trends throughout the data
